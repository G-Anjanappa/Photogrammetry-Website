ABOUT OUR WEBSITE

The photogrammetric product developed on this website is a Digital Surface Model (DSM) using the process of aerial image matching. The DSM explains the varying height of features such as buildings, trees, and other artefacts above the ground of a part of Boditi, Ethiopia. The product is available in two forms: a vector representation of DSM with varying colours, each depicting a specific height and a raster representation of DSM. This product is essential for applications such as flood modelling, 3D city modelling, and crisis management.

Digital Terrain Model (DTM)

DTM can be described as a 3D representation of a terrain surface consisting of X, Y, Z coordinates stored in digital form. It includes not only heights and elevations but other geographical elements and natural features (such as rivers, mountains etc.).


Digital Surface Model (DSM)

An elevation model that includes the tops of buildings, trees, powerlines, and any other objects. Commonly this is seen as a canopy model and only 'sees' ground where there is nothing else covering it.
